What is the Tridentine Mass?

The Tridentine Mass, or formally called the Tridentine rite is the Latin Roman rite revised and established for all the Western Church by Pope St. Pius V after the Council of Trent (1545 - 1563).

The word "Tridentine"" came from the Latin "Tridentinus", adjective from the name of the town of Trent, in Northern Italy.

The Tridentine rite remains a deeply cherished liturgy celebrated in Latin according to the 1962 Missal of Pope St. John XXIII.
